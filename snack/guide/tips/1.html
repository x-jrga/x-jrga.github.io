A Bit Of Information About Snack and Its Use
  • These are simple examples
  • I usually specify only a few constraints
  • Fiber, complete protein and calories constraints are good ones to start with
  • Snack will always give you the food combination that is lowest in calories
  • Snack may find the solution unfeasible
  • Watch out for constraints working against each other
  • When you get the solution unfeasible message try:
  • changing = for > or <
  • deleting constraints
  • adding extra food items
  • Any food item (diet or not) that has a nutritional label will work

Most of the time I eat the same things so I do not use Snack everyday. I only use Snack whenever I want to try a new food item or whenever I want to try a completely new menu to make sure I am within budget (calories) and to make sure I get the essential nutrients. I also use Snack to create new food items.

Snack Solution Testing Process That I Follow
  • Get a solution from Snack (food items and quantities)
  • Try it out for at least a week
  • If it works for you keep, if not, make adjustments, recalculate, get updated solution and try it again

The Way I Track Progress
  • Check body weight
  • Check body fat (I just do a quick check)
  • Pay attention to daily energy level
  • Pay attention to digestive system changes
  • Pay attention to mood changes
  • Pay attention to mental performance
  • Check vital signs (body temperature, pulse rate. respiration rate, blood pressure)
  • Have blood lab work done every few months to track changes

When trying to lose a few pounds as recommended by the experts, we must pay attention to the gap. The gap is the difference between the calorie intake where you don't gain or lose any weight (maintenance point) and whatever other calorie intake you compare it with. The larger the calorie gap you choose the harder it becomes to continue and the faster you could reach optimal weight. There are biological and psychological reasons for this. It is a tradeoff between discomfort and time (more discomfort, less time it takes to reach goal and viceversa). That is one of the reasons why experts recommend taking a gradual, lifelong approach to this process. Exercise increases the gap. If calorie intake is greater than maintenance point calorie intake then extra energy will accumulate as fat. The larger the reserve the longer it will take to deplete it.

You will feel and notice the changes. Once you get a solution that works for you then it is just a matter of patience, time and just a little discipline (I am assuming you have the right materials and equipment to work with). I learned much going through this process and got wonderful rewards attaining one small goal at a time. This is what I have experienced and I had much fun doing this.

Complementary things that will help you try solution

  • Measuring spoons and cups
  • Graduated beakers

Suggestions
  • Follow Dietary Guidelines and Recommendations created by Health Organizations
  • Speak with your doctor before starting a new diet or exercise program
